The Tainted Cup & A Drop of Corruption by Robert Jackson Bennett

Rating: 9/10

Robert Jackson Bennett knows how to plant a seed and make you forget about it until it blooms into something devastating.

The Tainted Cup and its sequel A Drop of Corruption are fantasy mysteries done right. The foreshadowing in these books is meticulous. Details that seem throwaway early on become pivotal later. That lyre in the opening chapters? You’ll see it again at the climax, and you’ll kick yourself for not seeing it coming.

The Hundred Thousand Kingdoms by N.K. Jemisin

Rating: 9/10

If you’ve read enough fantasy, you know the feeling. Another medieval kingdom, another chosen one, another magic system that feels like a reskin of the last three books you read. The Hundred Thousand Kingdoms isn’t that.

N.K. Jemisin built something genuinely original here. The setting alone sets it apart: gods (actual gods) enslaved and living among mortals, wielded as weapons by the ruling family. It’s a premise that sounds wild on paper and somehow works even better on the page.
