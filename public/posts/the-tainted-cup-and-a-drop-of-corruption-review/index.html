The Tainted Cup & A Drop of Corruption by Robert Jackson Bennett

February 9, 2026

Rating: 9/10

Robert Jackson Bennett knows how to plant a seed and make you forget about it until it blooms into something devastating.

The Tainted Cup and its sequel A Drop of Corruption are fantasy mysteries done right. The foreshadowing in these books is meticulous. Details that seem throwaway early on become pivotal later. That lyre in the opening chapters? You’ll see it again at the climax, and you’ll kick yourself for not seeing it coming.

These books are suspenseful in the best way. I was constantly on edge, turning pages because I needed to know what happens next. Bennett keeps the tension wound tight without it ever feeling exhausting.

The worldbuilding pulls its weight too. The fantasy elements are fresh and strange in ways that make you hungry for more lore. The leviathans loom over everything, massive and mysterious, and the books dole out just enough information to keep you hooked without ever fully satisfying your curiosity.

And then there’s Ana. You spend the whole time trying to figure out what she actually is, what she can really do. Bennett plays that mystery perfectly.

If you like fantasy that makes you think while keeping you on the edge of your seat, these deliver.
